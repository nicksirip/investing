## Understanding the Global Recession Spectrum

Global recessions are defined by simultaneous contractions across multiple major economies. Unlike country-specific downturns, synchronized global recessions are amplified by trade linkages, capital flow reversals, and coordinated credit tightening. Positioning must account for currency dynamics, EM vs. DM divergence, and commodity price dislocations.

| Level | Description | Example Events |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1 – Synchronized Expansion** | Broad global growth, rising trade volumes, EM inflows | Global GDP >3%, IMF upgrades forecasts, commodity supercycle |
| **2 – Coordinated Slowdown** | Growth decelerating across multiple major economies | Global PMI declining, China slowdown, US/EU divergence narrowing |
| **3 – Mild Global Recession** | GDP contraction in two or more G7 economies; EM stress | Trade volumes falling, EM capital outflows, USD strengthening broadly |
| **4 – Severe Global Recession** | Broad GDP contraction, financial system stress across regions | Credit freezes, EM debt crises, commodity demand collapse |
| **5 – Global Depression / Systemic Crisis** | Multi-year global contraction, sovereign defaults, systemic bank failures | Unemployment >10% in major economies, deflation, IMF emergency programs |

## Key Risk Factors to Monitor

### Global Macroeconomic Indicators
- IMF World Economic Outlook GDP growth projections (biannual)
- JP Morgan Global Manufacturing PMI (monthly composite)
- OECD Composite Leading Indicators (CLI) across major economies
- World Trade Organization (WTO) global goods trade volume index
- G20 consumer confidence and retail sales trends
- Global freight indices: Baltic Dry Index (BDI), Freightos Baltic Index (FBX)

### Credit & Sovereign Risk
- Emerging market sovereign spreads (EMBI Global, JPMorgan)
- European peripheral spreads (Italy/Germany 10Y BTP-Bund spread)
- Global high-yield credit spreads (ICE BofA Global HY Index)
- IMF special drawing rights (SDR) allocations and emergency lending programs
- Sovereign credit rating actions from Moody's, S&P, and Fitch on major EM economies
- Cross-currency basis swaps (USD funding stress indicator)

### Currency & Capital Flow Signals
- US Dollar Index (DXY) trend — USD strength accelerates EM stress
- EM currency basket (CEW) performance vs. USD
- China CNY/USD exchange rate and PBOC intervention signals
- Japanese yen (JPY) as safe-haven barometer in global risk-off periods
- Capital flow data: IIF Global Capital Flows Tracker (monthly)
- Cross-border bank lending data (BIS quarterly)

### Commodity Markets
- Brent crude oil price — global demand barometer
- Copper price (LME) — leading indicator of industrial demand ("Dr. Copper")
- Iron ore price (SGX) — China construction and infrastructure demand
- CRB Commodity Index — broad basket of global commodities
- Agricultural commodities (soybeans, wheat) — food security and EM inflation signal

## Individual Stock Considerations

### Long Candidates

**Global Consumer Staples:**
- **PG (Procter & Gamble)** – Sells in 180+ countries; pricing power and brand loyalty across economic cycles; 60+ consecutive years of dividend increases
- **KO (Coca-Cola)** – Dominant global beverage distribution; resilient through every global recession; pricing power across EM and DM markets
- **UL (Unilever)** – Significant EM consumer staples exposure that diversifies geography; defensive earnings profile

**Global Healthcare:**
- **JNJ (Johnson & Johnson)** – Diversified pharma and MedTech with global revenue base; AAA credit rating; fortress balance sheet
- **NVO (Novo Nordisk)** – Structural growth in diabetes and obesity treatments globally; Denmark-listed ADR with non-US revenue base
- **AZN (AstraZeneca)** – Global pharmaceutical with significant EM revenue diversification; pipeline depth provides earnings resilience

**Safe-Haven / Defensive:**
- **TLT** – 20+ year US Treasury ETF; primary global safe-haven instrument; rallies as global risk-off demand for USD assets surges
- **GLD** – Gold ETF; global store of value that outperforms in synchronized global contractions and USD stress events
- **ACWV** – MSCI World minimum volatility ETF; provides global equity exposure with downside mitigation factor

### Short / Avoid Candidates

- **EM consumer discretionary and tech (BABA, Samsung, Mercado Libre)** – Capital outflows and currency depreciation erode USD-denominated returns; domestic demand collapses
- **Global commodity producers (BHP, RIO, VALE, FCX)** – Demand destruction hits mining and metals hardest in global recessions
- **European financials with peripheral exposure (DB, UniCredit, Santander)** – Cross-border loan losses, sovereign bond holdings at risk, and interbank stress
- **Airlines and global travel (LUV, DAL, IAG, Lufthansa)** – Business and leisure travel collapse simultaneously; high fixed-cost structures amplify losses
- **Luxury goods (LVMH, Kering, Burberry)** – Ultra-high-net-worth demand resilient but aspirational luxury spending collapses; China exposure adds EM risk

## Hedging Strategies

### Options-Based Hedges

1. **EEM put spreads** – Buy ATM put / sell OTM put on emerging markets ETF; EM equities typically lead the drawdown and fall furthest in global recessions
2. **SPY put spreads** – Broad US market hedge; defined-risk structure; roll quarterly as recession deepens
3. **HYG put options** – Global credit canary; HYG breakdown signals broader cross-border credit deterioration
4. **EMB put options** – EM sovereign bond ETF; USD-denominated EM debt stress hedge
5. **EWI / EWP puts** – European peripheral equity stress; sovereign spread widening hedge

### Interest Rate & Currency Plays

- **Long TLT / IEF** – US Treasury duration extension; flight-to-safety demand surges globally in recessions
- **Long IGOV (international sovereign bonds)** – German Bunds and Japanese JGBs rally in global risk-off
- **Long JPY (FXY)** – Japanese yen is the world's premier safe-haven currency; appreciates sharply as global carry trades unwind
- **Long CHF (FXF)** – Swiss franc safe-haven demand; SNB intervention risk limits upside but CHF broadly appreciates
- **Long USD (UUP)** – Dollar milkshake effect: global USD funding demand spikes in recessions, pulling capital into US assets
- **Short EM currencies** – Brazilian real (BRL), South African rand (ZAR), Turkish lira (TRY) typically fall sharply vs. USD

### Volatility & Commodity Hedges

- **Long VIX calls (60–90 day tenor)** – Global recession onset causes sharp synchronized VIX spikes across equity markets
- **Long GLD** – Gold outperforms in global recessions as central banks globally ease simultaneously; diversifies against currency debasement
- **Short crude oil (via USO puts or SCO)** – Global demand destruction hits oil prices; DXY strength amplifies downward pressure
- **Short copper (CPER puts)** – Dr. Copper is the best real-time barometer of global industrial demand deterioration

## Scenario-Based Positioning

### Scenario A: Coordinated Slowdown (Level 2)

**Probability driver:** Global PMI falling toward 50, IMF downgrading world GDP to <2.5%, China growth slowing below 4%, US and European data softening

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Trim EM equity exposure 5–10% (EEM, VWO) | Capital outflows begin; EM most vulnerable to early-cycle global slowdown |
| Add global defensive equities (ACWV, EFAV) | Shift to minimum-volatility global factor |
| Begin USD accumulation (UUP) 3–5% | Dollar tends to strengthen as global growth slows |
| Add GLD 3–5% | Early safe-haven hedging; low cost at this stage |
| Add IGOV or TLT 5% | Begin duration extension as rate cut expectations build globally |
| Reduce commodity exposure (energy, metals) | Global demand softening hits commodity prices |

### Scenario B: Mild Global Recession (Level 3)

**Probability driver:** Two or more G7 economies contracting, global PMI sustained below 48, EM capital outflows accelerating, USD DXY above 105

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Reduce EM to 0–5% total portfolio | Outflows, currency depreciation, and sovereign stress make EM unownable |
| Increase global defensives (XLP, XLV, ACWV) to 20–25% | Core defensive positioning across geographies |
| Add TLT 10%, IGOV 5% | Rate cut cycle begins globally; long-duration benefits |
| Increase GLD to 8–10% | Coordinated central bank easing supports gold |
| Buy EEM put spreads (3–6 month tenor) | Defined-risk EM tail hedge |
| Short HYG / buy HYG puts | Global credit deterioration play |
| Increase USD exposure (UUP) to 5–8% | Dollar strengthens as global risk-off accelerates |

### Scenario C: Severe Global Recession (Level 4)

**Probability driver:** GDP contraction across US, EU, China, and Japan simultaneously; EM sovereign stress/defaults; global trade volumes -10% or worse; oil below $50

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum defensive allocation — XLP, XLV, XLU 25–30% combined | Capital preservation priority globally |
| Increase TLT to 15–20%; add IGOV | Aggressive synchronized rate cuts; flight to safety surges |
| Increase GLD to 10–15% | Deep global uncertainty; simultaneous central bank easing debases all fiat |
| Exit EM entirely; long EUM (inverse EM) | EM capital flight and potential sovereign defaults |
| Long JPY (FXY) and CHF (FXF) to 5% combined | Safe-haven currency appreciation as carry trades unwind globally |
| Long USD (UUP) 8–10% | Dollar milkshake effect at maximum intensity |
| Buy VIX calls (60–90 day); buy SPY / EEM puts | Volatility hedge through peak global distress |
| Short global commodities (crude oil, copper, iron ore) | Global demand destruction; no floor until policy response materializes |

### Scenario D: Global Recovery / Expansion Re-Entry (Level 1–2)

**Probability driver:** Global PMI recovering above 50, IMF upgrading growth forecasts, EM capital inflows resuming, commodity prices stabilizing, coordinated central bank easing gaining traction

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Gradually re-add EM equities (EEM, VWO) 5–10% | EM outperforms strongly in early global recovery as capital returns |
| Re-add global cyclicals (industrials, materials) | Global trade recovery drives manufacturing and commodity demand |
| Add global small caps selectively | Small caps outperform in early recovery phase |
| Trim TLT / IGOV; shift to shorter duration | Yields rise as global recovery priced in |
| Reduce GLD allocation | Risk-on environment reduces safe-haven demand |
| Unwind USD position (reduce UUP) | Dollar weakens as global risk appetite recovers |
| Reduce JPY / CHF exposure | Safe-haven demand deflates; carry trades rebuild |
| Re-add commodity ETFs (DBC, PDBC) | Industrial demand recovery supports broad commodities |

## Key Indicators & Triggers

### Early Warning Signals (Begin Defensive Positioning)
- JP Morgan Global Manufacturing PMI falling below 50 for two consecutive months
- IMF World Economic Outlook global GDP forecast downgraded below 2.5%
- Baltic Dry Index (BDI) declining more than 30% from recent peak
- EM capital outflows accelerating (IIF Global Capital Flows Tracker turning negative)
- Copper price declining more than 20% from recent peak ("Dr. Copper" recession signal)
- US yield curve inversion (10Y/2Y) sustained for 3+ months — historically precedes global recessions
- European peripheral spread widening (Italy/Germany 10Y BTP-Bund spread exceeding 250bps)

### Recession Confirmation Signals (Full Defensive Positioning)
- GDP contraction in two or more G7 economies confirmed in official data releases
- Global PMI sustained below 48 for three or more consecutive months
- EM sovereign credit downgrades by two or more major rating agencies (Moody's, S&P, Fitch)
- Global trade volumes declining more than 5% year-over-year (WTO data)
- IMF activating emergency lending facilities for multiple major EM economies
- Cross-currency basis swaps signaling acute USD funding stress (basis more negative than -50bps)
- Brent crude oil declining below $50/barrel on global demand destruction

### Recovery / Re-Entry Signals (Begin Rotating to Cyclicals)
- Global Manufacturing PMI recovering above 50 for two consecutive months
- IMF upgrading global GDP forecasts two quarters in a row
- Baltic Dry Index recovering more than 20% from trough
- EM capital inflows resuming (IIF Tracker turning positive for three consecutive months)
- Copper price recovering more than 15% from recession trough
- EM currencies (CEW) stabilizing and beginning to appreciate vs. USD
- Global corporate earnings revisions turning net positive
