## Understanding the Collapse Spectrum

Mortgage lender stress ranges from isolated margin pressures to full systemic contagion through the banking system and housing market. Positioning should shift gradually as conditions evolve.

| Level | Description | Example Events |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1 – Stable** | Normal mortgage market conditions | Moderate delinquency rates, healthy origination volumes, tight credit spreads |
| **2 – Elevated Stress** | Rising delinquencies, origination slowdown | Mortgage delinquency rates rising, non-bank lenders tightening standards, MBS spreads widening |
| **3 – Lender Distress** | Individual lender failures, liquidity crises | Mid-size mortgage companies filing for bankruptcy, warehouse line draws frozen, MSR valuations falling |
| **4 – Sector Contagion** | Wave of lender failures, GSE stress | Multiple large lenders failing, Fannie/Freddie spread widening, regional bank loan losses mounting |
| **5 – Systemic Crisis** | Full credit freeze, government intervention | GSE conservatorship, Federal Reserve emergency facilities, widespread bank failures |

## Key Risk Factors to Monitor

### Mortgage Market Indicators
- MBA Mortgage Delinquency Survey (30-, 60-, 90-day and seriously delinquent rates)
- Mortgage Bankers Association (MBA) Weekly Applications Survey — purchase and refinance volumes
- Non-QM and jumbo mortgage spread versus conforming loan rates
- Warehouse lending line utilization rates at non-bank mortgage companies
- Mortgage servicing rights (MSR) valuation trends

### Housing & Credit Market Signals
- Case-Shiller and FHFA Home Price Indices (monthly)
- Housing starts, building permits, and existing home sales (monthly Census Bureau / NAR)
- Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac MBS spreads versus Treasuries (OAS)
- ABX and CDS indices on mortgage-backed securities
- CMBS spreads and commercial real estate delinquency data

### Financial System & Regulatory
- Federal Reserve Senior Loan Officer Survey — residential mortgage lending standards
- FDIC bank examination reports and bank failure list
- Non-bank mortgage company earnings — origination margins, gain-on-sale margins, net income
- FHFA conservatorship risk indicators for Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac
- Treasury and FHFA announcements on housing finance reform

## Sector Playbook

### Overweight in Escalation Scenarios

| Sector | Rationale | Representative Names |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **US Treasuries & Investment-Grade Bonds** | Flight to safety; Fed rate cuts likely in systemic stress | TLT, IEF, AGG, BND |
| **Consumer Staples** | Defensive cash flows; resilient through housing downturns | PG, KO, WMT, COST |
| **Utilities** | Regulated earnings; uncorrelated to mortgage credit cycle | NEE, DUK, SO, AEP |
| **Gold & Hard Assets** | Store of value; hedge against financial system stress | GLD, IAU, NEM, PHYS |
| **Distressed Debt / Special Situations** | Opportunistic; collapsed lender asset portfolios trade at steep discounts | KCLI, PFSI (post-distress), opportunistic PE/hedge exposure |
| **GSE Reform Beneficiaries** | Crisis often catalyzes housing finance reform | Recapitalization plays in FNMA/FMCC preferreds (high risk) |
| **Property & Casualty Insurance** | Premium income uncorrelated to mortgage defaults; benefits from rising rates | CB, ALL, PGR, TRV |

### Underweight / Reduce in Escalation Scenarios

| Sector | Rationale | Representative Names |
|--------|-----------|---------------------|
| **Non-Bank Mortgage Originators** | Direct exposure to origination margin compression, warehouse line risk, and liquidity crises | RKT (Rocket), UWMC (UWM), PFSI (PennyMac), COOP (Mr. Cooper) |
| **Homebuilders** | Demand destruction as mortgage availability tightens; cancellation rates spike | DHI, LEN, PHM, TOL, NVR |
| **Regional & Community Banks** | Concentrated residential real estate loan portfolios; deposit flight risk | KRE constituents, NYCB, SBNY-type exposures |
| **Commercial REITs** | Contagion from residential to commercial; financing risk as credit tightens | VNQ (selectively), SLG, BXP, KREF |
| **Mortgage REITs** | Directly impacted by spread widening, margin calls, book value declines | NLY, AGNC, TWO, MFA, RWT |
| **Big-4 Banks (selectively)** | Mortgage loss provisioning, trading losses on MBS, reputational risk | BAC, WFC (historically most exposed); reduce on deterioration signals |

## Individual Stock Considerations

### Long Candidates

**Treasuries & Defensive Fixed Income:**
- **TLT** – 20+ year Treasury ETF; rallies on flight to safety and rate cut expectations during mortgage crises; core holding in stress scenarios

**Consumer Staples:**
- **PG (Procter & Gamble)** – Household essentials; recession-proof revenues; pricing power and 60+ year dividend growth streak
- **WMT (Walmart)** – Benefits from trade-down as housing wealth effect weakens; grocery and essentials scale

**Utilities:**
- **NEE (NextEra Energy)** – Regulated earnings base with renewable pipeline; dividend growth uncorrelated to mortgage cycle
- **DUK (Duke Energy)** – Stable regulated utility; Southeast US exposure with low cyclicality

**Gold:**
- **NEM (Newmont)** – Largest gold miner; liquid proxy for gold in financial system stress scenarios

### Short / Avoid Candidates

- **RKT (Rocket Companies)** – Largest non-bank mortgage originator; origination volume collapses with rate/demand shock; gain-on-sale margins compress to zero or negative
- **UWMC (UWM Holdings)** – Wholesale mortgage channel highly exposed to rate volatility and warehouse line risk
- **NLY (Annaly Capital Management)** – Largest mortgage REIT; leveraged MBS portfolio subject to margin calls and book value destruction in spread widening
- **AGNC Investment Corp** – Agency MBS REIT; dividend cuts follow spread widening and leverage unwind
- **NYCB (or equivalent community bank with concentrated CRE/residential exposure)** – Repeat of 2023 NYCB stress: concentrated real estate loan portfolios vulnerable to mark-to-market losses
- **DHI / LEN (D.R. Horton / Lennar)** – Homebuilder earnings collapse with affordability destruction; cancellation rates spike in tight-credit environments

## Hedging Strategies

### Options-Based Hedges

1. **XHB put spreads** – Buy ATM put / sell OTM put on SPDR S&P Homebuilders; defined-risk hedge on housing demand collapse; 3–6 month tenor
2. **KRE put options** – Regional banking stress is a leading indicator; 5–10% OTM puts with 90–180 day tenor
3. **REM put options** – Mortgage REIT basket most directly exposed to MBS spread widening; relatively cheap during complacent periods
4. **RKT / UWMC put options** – Targeted short exposure to non-bank originators; consider put spreads for defined risk
5. **SPY put spreads** – Broad market hedge for systemic contagion scenario; roll quarterly

### Interest Rate Plays

- **Long TLT / IEF** – Duration extension; Fed typically cuts aggressively in mortgage crises (2008 precedent)
- **Long TIPS (TIP)** – If crisis is accompanied by stagflationary dynamics (supply shock + credit freeze)
- **Short MBS spread trade** – Buy TLT / short agency MBS (MBB) spread trade captures widening OAS on agency mortgage-backed securities

### Currency & Volatility Hedges

- **Long USD (UUP)** – Dollar strengthens in global risk-off events; historical pattern during mortgage/credit crises
- **Long JPY** – Traditional safe-haven currency; appreciates as US financial stress drives risk-off sentiment
- **Long VIX calls or call spreads** – Mortgage lender failures cause sharp VIX spikes; 30–60 day tenor for event hedges
- **Long UVXY** – Leveraged VIX ETF for short-term event hedges; significant decay makes this a short-duration trade only

## Scenario-Based Positioning

### Scenario A: Rising Delinquencies / Origination Slowdown (Level 2)

**Probability driver:** MBA delinquency data rising, non-bank lenders reporting margin compression, MBS spreads widening 25–50bps

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Trim homebuilder exposure (DHI, LEN, PHM) 50% | Leading indicator of demand destruction |
| Add XLP, XLU 5% | Begin building defensive allocation |
| Add IEF 5% | Partial rate cut benefit as Fed responds |
| Buy XHB put spreads (3–6 month) | Defined-risk hedge; relatively cheap before stress peaks |
| Reduce mortgage REIT exposure (NLY, AGNC) | MBS spread widening compresses book value |

### Scenario B: Individual Lender Failures (Level 3)

**Probability driver:** Non-bank mortgage company bankruptcy filings, warehouse lines frozen, MSR valuations in freefall

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Exit non-bank originator stocks (RKT, UWMC, COOP) | Direct existential risk; equity goes to zero in failures |
| Increase KRE put options | Regional bank contagion from mortgage losses |
| Add TLT 10% | Rate cut cycle begins; long duration benefits |
| Add GLD 5% | Financial system uncertainty; safe haven demand |
| Short REM or buy REM puts | Mortgage REIT sector repricing |
| Reduce broad financials (XLF) to underweight | Contagion risk to broader banking sector |

### Scenario C: GSE Stress / Wave of Failures (Level 4)

**Probability driver:** Fannie/Freddie MBS spreads widening 100bps+, FHFA intervention signals, multiple large lender failures

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum defensive positioning (XLP, XLV, XLU) 25–30% | Capital preservation priority |
| Increase TLT to 15–20% | Aggressive Fed rate cuts; 2008-style flight to safety |
| Add GLD 10% | Deep financial system uncertainty |
| Exit or heavily short homebuilders (XHB) | Housing market collapse scenario |
| Long USD (UUP) | Risk-off dollar strengthening |
| Buy VIX calls (60–90 day) | Volatility hedge through peak distress |
| Buy KRE puts aggressively | Regional bank failures follow mortgage lender wave |

### Scenario D: Stabilization / Government Intervention (Level 1–2)

**Probability driver:** Fed announces emergency facilities, FHFA/Treasury backstops GSEs, delinquency data plateaus

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Gradually re-add homebuilders (DHI, NVR) | Stabilization and government backstop removes tail risk |
| Trim TLT; shift to intermediate duration (IEF) | Yields rise as crisis premium fades |
| Reduce GLD allocation | Risk-on; safe havens deflate |
| Selectively re-add regional banks (KRE) | Survivors re-rate higher post-crisis |
| Unwind XHB and KRE hedges | Hedge cost no longer justified |
| Evaluate distressed mortgage servicer recovery plays | Post-crisis asset consolidators can be compelling longs |

## Key Indicators & Triggers

### Early Warning Signals (Begin Defensive Positioning)
- MBA serious delinquency rate (90+ days) rising above 2% from cycle lows
- Non-bank mortgage originator gain-on-sale margins turning negative for two consecutive quarters
- MBS OAS (option-adjusted spread) widening more than 50bps in 30 days
- Warehouse lending lines being reduced or withdrawn at 2+ major non-bank lenders
- FHFA Home Price Index declining month-over-month for three consecutive months
- MBA Purchase Applications Index declining more than 20% year-over-year

### Distress Confirmation Signals (Full Defensive Positioning)
- Non-bank mortgage company bankruptcy or FDIC-assisted resolution announced
- Fannie Mae or Freddie Mac MBS guarantee fees materially increased or spreads blow out
- Federal Reserve activating emergency credit facilities for mortgage market (e.g., TALF-style)
- Regional bank earnings guidance cut sharply due to residential mortgage loan loss provisioning
- FDIC bank failure list expanding at 10+ institutions per quarter
- ABX CDS index deteriorating rapidly (similar to 2007–2008 pattern)

### Recovery / Re-Entry Signals (Begin Rotating Back to Sector)
- MBA delinquency rate declining for three consecutive quarters from peak
- Non-bank originator gain-on-sale margins recovering above 150bps
- MBS spreads tightening toward historical norms (OAS below 30bps on agency MBS)
- Housing inventory stabilizing and price declines slowing in CoreLogic / Case-Shiller data
- Federal Reserve language signaling end of emergency support (crisis resolved)
- Surviving mortgage lenders reporting origination volume growth year-over-year
