## Understanding the Conflict Spectrum

A global world war involves simultaneous military conflict across multiple theaters — Europe, the Indo-Pacific, the Middle East, and potentially Africa — drawing in the world's major military powers (US, NATO, Russia, China) and their allies. The spectrum progresses from escalating regional proxy conflicts through full multi-theater mobilization and, at the extreme, nuclear exchange. Positioning must evolve across each phase, shifting from growth exposure toward hard assets, defense, and capital preservation as the situation intensifies.

| Level | Description | Example Events |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1 – Elevated Tensions** | Simultaneous regional crises across multiple theaters; no direct great-power conflict | Russia-Ukraine war ongoing, Taiwan Strait naval standoff, Middle East escalation, diplomatic breakdown between US and China |
| **2 – Proxy Escalation** | Major powers supporting opposing sides in multiple conflicts; coordinated economic warfare | US/NATO vs. Russia proxy escalation, China supplying Russia, Iran activating regional proxies, synchronized cyberattacks on Western infrastructure |
| **3 – Limited Great-Power Engagement** | Direct military engagement between at least two major powers in a contained theater | US-China naval clash over Taiwan, NATO-Russia direct confrontation in Eastern Europe, coordinated air and missile exchanges |
| **4 – Multi-Theater War** | Active armed conflict across two or more major theaters involving multiple great powers | Simultaneous NATO-Russia war in Europe and US-China war in the Pacific, allied nations mobilizing reserves, war economy footing |
| **5 – Total / Nuclear** | Full-scale global mobilization; nuclear weapons used or imminent | Strategic nuclear exchange, DEFCON 1, global economic shutdown, capital markets halted |

## Key Risk Factors to Monitor

### Military & Geopolitical
- Simultaneous escalation across multiple theaters (Europe, Indo-Pacific, Middle East)
- Great-power diplomatic communication breakdown (hotlines severed, ambassadors expelled)
- Nuclear posture changes by US, Russia, or China (readiness alerts, doctrine statements, deployment of tactical nuclear assets)
- Activation of NATO Article 5 or US mutual defense treaties with Asian allies (ANZUS, US-Japan, US-South Korea)
- UN Security Council paralysis due to vetoes from permanent members involved in conflict
- Large-scale troop mobilizations, conscription announcements, or reserve call-ups by major powers
- Cyber and satellite warfare escalation (GPS disruption, financial system attacks, critical infrastructure targeting)

### Energy & Commodities
- Global oil and natural gas supply disruption (Strait of Hormuz, Strait of Malacca blockades)
- Russian and Middle Eastern energy export suspension or interdiction
- Rare earth and critical mineral supply cutoff from China (rare earths, gallium, germanium, graphite)
- Agricultural supply shock — Ukraine/Russia wheat, Southeast Asian rice, and key fertilizer supply chains disrupted simultaneously
- Shipping lane closure risk (Red Sea, South China Sea, Black Sea, Mediterranean) affecting global trade
- Strategic petroleum reserve drawdowns and emergency energy rationing in Western nations

### Financial / Market
- US Dollar surging to multi-decade highs as global safe haven (DXY index)
- Gold and silver in extreme backwardation; physical premium over paper price
- Global equity markets in freefall across multiple major exchanges
- Credit markets freezing: investment-grade and high-yield spreads widening sharply
- Currency collapse in nations directly involved in conflict (EUR, JPY, CNY, RUB volatility)
- Emergency Federal Reserve, ECB, and Bank of Japan interventions; potential capital controls in affected nations
- Foreign exchange reserves of adversary nations targeted via sanctions (SWIFT exclusion, reserve freezes)

### Policy & Institutional
- US Presidential declaration of national emergency; wartime economic powers invoked (Defense Production Act)
- Allied nations' defense spending commitments surge past 3–4% of GDP
- UN General Assembly emergency sessions; international institution paralysis
- Trade and financial sanctions packages expanding to cover multiple adversary nations
- Emergency G7/G20 economic coordination; wartime financial architecture (e.g., Lend-Lease-style programs)

## Individual Stock Considerations

### Long Candidates

**US Defense Contractors:**
- **LMT (Lockheed Martin)** – F-35 fighter jet, HIMARS rocket systems, Javelin anti-tank systems; primary supplier to US and allied militaries globally; backlog surges in multi-theater war
- **RTX (Raytheon Technologies)** – Patriot air and missile defense systems; Stinger MANPADS; Tomahawk cruise missiles; AIM-120 AMRAAM; indispensable to NATO air defense
- **NOC (Northrop Grumman)** – B-21 Raider stealth bomber; nuclear intercontinental ballistic missile (GBSD/Sentinel) program; strategic deterrence and long-range strike
- **GD (General Dynamics)** – Abrams tanks; Virginia-class submarines; Gulfstream jets used by militaries; Stryker combat vehicles; naval systems critical for multi-ocean war
- **HII (Huntington Ingalls Industries)** – Sole builder of US Navy aircraft carriers; largest military shipbuilder; critical to Pacific theater naval expansion
- **LHX (L3Harris Technologies)** – Electronic warfare, communications, ISR systems; modern warfighting command-and-control backbone
- **LDOS (Leidos)** – Defense IT, intelligence systems, and advanced analytics; critical for wartime logistics and intelligence operations

**Cybersecurity:**
- **CRWD (CrowdStrike)** – Government and critical infrastructure security; protecting military and intelligence networks from state-sponsored attackers
- **PANW (Palo Alto Networks)** – Network security and zero-trust architecture; major contracts with US DoD and allied governments

**Energy:**
- **LNG (Cheniere Energy)** – Largest US LNG exporter; European and Asian allies rely on US LNG as strategic energy alternative to adversary suppliers
- **XOM (ExxonMobil)** – Diversified global energy; elevated oil prices benefit free cash flow; domestic US production critical to allied energy security
- **CVX (Chevron)** – Similar diversified energy profile; Permian Basin production insulated from conflict zone supply disruptions

**Commodities & Materials:**
- **CCJ (Cameco)** – Largest Western uranium miner; nuclear energy expansion accelerates as war disrupts fossil fuel supply chains
- **ADM (Archer-Daniels-Midland)** – Global grain trader and processor; benefits from agricultural supply disruption across multiple conflict zones
- **NEM (Newmont)** – World's largest gold miner; direct beneficiary of gold safe-haven surge

### Short / Avoid Candidates

- **Internationally exposed airlines (DAL, UAL, IAG, LHA)** – Airspace closures, fuel cost spikes, demand collapse
- **China-dependent tech hardware (AAPL, QCOM)** – Semiconductor supply chain disruption; potential sanctions or asset seizure risk in conflict with China
- **Global luxury goods (LVMH, EL)** – Consumer sentiment collapse; Chinese consumer demand evaporation
- **European auto manufacturers (VOW3, BMW, STE)** – Input cost shock, energy crisis, demand destruction, supply chain collapse
- **Broad international and emerging market equity (EEM, VWO, EFA)** – Conflict discount, capital flight, currency risk
- **Global shipping and logistics (ZIM, MAERSK.B)** – Maritime security risk; shipping lane closures

## Hedging Strategies

### Options-Based Hedges

1. **SPY / QQQ put spreads** – Broad US equity hedge; global war is the most severe macro shock; buy ATM put / sell OTM put; 3–6 month tenor to cover escalation cycle
2. **EEM / EFA put spreads** – International equity hedge; emerging and developed markets most vulnerable to capital flight in multi-theater conflict
3. **EZU / VGK puts** – European equity hedge; European theater of war creates recession and market closure risk
4. **MCHI puts** – China equity hedge; US-China conflict causes rapid capital flight from Chinese equities and potential asset freeze
5. **VIX calls** – Volatility spike hedge; purchase 30–90 day VIX calls as war risk escalates; roll regularly to avoid decay
6. **XLE / LNG call spreads** – Defined-risk energy price upside capture; oil and LNG spike on supply disruption

### Currency Hedges

- **Long USD (UUP)** – US Dollar is the global safe haven in any scenario short of a US sovereign crisis; strengthens dramatically in global risk-off
- **Long Gold (GLD / IAU)** – Universal monetary safe haven; outperforms all currencies in existential geopolitical scenarios; holds value even when USD is stressed
- **Long CHF** – Swiss franc is traditional global conflict safe haven; Switzerland's neutrality supports value even in European war
- **Short EUR (FXE puts / EUO)** – European theater of war devastates the Euro; energy crisis, recession, and capital controls weaken EUR
- **Short CNY (CNY futures or FXI puts)** – US-China conflict triggers capital outflows, sanctions, and potential Yuan devaluation
- **Short JPY (long USD/JPY)** – Japan is a front-line ally in Pacific theater; defense spending surge and economic disruption weaken JPY

### Commodity Hedges

- **Long GLD / physical gold** – Maximum safe-haven allocation; war premium is highest across all commodity hedges
- **Long DBO / USO** – Crude oil price spike on global supply disruption; Strait of Hormuz and other chokepoint risk
- **Long UNG** – Natural gas supply disruption from European theater and Middle East conflict
- **Long DBA / WEAT** – Global food security crisis; multiple agricultural exporters simultaneously disrupted
- **Long URA / CCJ** – Uranium/nuclear energy demand spike as fossil fuels become strategic liabilities

### Tax-Efficiency Considerations

- **Defense ETFs (ITA, XAR, PPA) in taxable accounts** — Hold long-term (>1 year) for preferential long-term capital gains rates; relatively low annual distributions
- **Gold (GLD, IAU)** — Taxed as collectibles (28% max rate); hold in IRA/Roth IRA to eliminate the collectible rate penalty; gold miners (GDX, NEM) taxed as equities at standard capital gains rates
- **Commodity ETFs (DBO, UNG, DBA)** — Mark-to-market (Section 1256) taxation at blended 60/40 long/short-term rate; consider holding within IRA if turnover is high
- **VIX calls and index options** — Section 1256 treatment (60/40 blended rate); generally favorable tax treatment on gains vs. equity options
- **Inverse / leveraged ETFs (SH, SDS)** — Typically generate short-term gains; hold in tax-advantaged accounts to avoid ordinary income treatment
- **Foreign equity puts (EZU, EEM)** — Short-term gains if tenor under 12 months; time exits strategically to maximize long-term treatment where possible

## Scenario-Based Positioning

### Scenario A: Elevated Multi-Theater Tensions (Level 1–2)

**Probability driver:** Simultaneous regional conflicts, coordinated economic warfare, great-power diplomatic breakdown

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Add ITA, XAR 5–8% | Defense spending surge across all major powers; weapons stockpile depletion accelerates procurement |
| Add GLD 5–8% | Early safe-haven accumulation before war premium is fully priced in |
| Add XLE, LNG 3–5% | Energy supply risk premium; begin building energy exposure ahead of potential chokepoint closures |
| Add CIBR, CRWD 2–3% | Coordinated state-sponsored cyberattacks on Western infrastructure accelerate |
| Trim EEM, EFA 5–10% | International equity discount; capital flight from geopolitical hot spots |
| Buy SPY put spreads (3–6 month) | Tail risk hedge for escalation to direct great-power conflict |
| Add DBA, WEAT 2–3% | Early agricultural commodity exposure as multi-region food supply chains become vulnerable |

### Scenario B: Limited Great-Power Engagement (Level 3)

**Probability driver:** Direct US-China clash over Taiwan or NATO-Russia direct confrontation; first shots fired between major powers

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Increase ITA, XAR, LMT, RTX to 15–18% combined | Wartime procurement surge; weapons systems demand explodes; multi-year backlog visibility |
| Increase GLD, IAU to 12–15% | War premium fully priced in; maximum safe-haven allocation |
| Increase XLE, LNG, DBO to 10–12% | Supply disruption escalates; energy is critical wartime commodity |
| Eliminate or minimize EEM, EFA, EZU, MCHI | Direct conflict in Asia and Europe destroys international equity value |
| Buy EEM / MCHI put spreads (3–6 month) | Capital flight from conflict theaters; Chinese equity collapse risk |
| Add CRWD, PANW, CIBR 5% | Infrastructure cyberattacks peak; government cybersecurity contracts surge |
| Buy VIX calls (30–60 day) | Volatility spikes; roll regularly |
| Add URA, CCJ 3% | Nuclear energy buildout accelerates as energy security becomes critical national priority |
| Short EUR (FXE puts / EUO) | European theater conflict devastates EUR |
| Move 15–20% to SHY / T-bills | Capital preservation; maintain dry powder for post-conflict recovery opportunities |

### Scenario C: Multi-Theater World War (Level 4)

**Probability driver:** Simultaneous active conflict in Europe and Pacific; multiple great powers at war; wartime economic mobilization

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Maximum defense allocation (ITA, XAR, NOC, HII, LMT, RTX) 25%+ | War economy; defense spending becomes uncapped; defense contractors operate at maximum capacity |
| Maximum safe-haven (GLD, USD, T-bills) 25%+ | Capital preservation is the primary objective; geopolitical risk premium at maximum |
| Eliminate all international equity | War zones, capital controls, and exchange closures make international equity worthless or inaccessible |
| Maximum energy allocation (XLE, LNG, DBO, UNG) 15% | Global supply chain collapse; energy is the most critical wartime commodity |
| Hold 20%+ cash / T-bills | Extreme uncertainty; preserve optionality for post-war reconstruction opportunities |
| Long VIX (roll monthly) | Sustained extreme volatility; do not hold for extended periods due to decay |
| Long DBA, WEAT | Global food security crisis; agricultural prices spike as multiple breadbasket regions disrupted |
| Eliminate technology hardware with China supply chain exposure | Sanctions, component shortages, and potential asset seizures in conflict with China |

### Scenario D: De-escalation / Armistice (Level 1–2)

**Probability driver:** Ceasefire agreements, peace negotiations, diplomatic settlement, great-power communication restoration

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Gradually trim defense overweights | Peace dividend; defense spending peak passes; sector rotation begins |
| Selectively re-add international equity (EFA, VGK, EEM) | Post-war reconstruction and recovery opportunities; depressed valuations create entry points |
| Trim energy (XLE, LNG) gradually | Supply risk eases; energy premium deflates on conflict resolution |
| Reduce GLD allocation | Risk-on environment returns; safe havens deflate as geopolitical premium compresses |
| Add European and Asian infrastructure / industrials | Reconstruction spending benefits; steel, cement, engineering, infrastructure ETFs |
| Add emerging market reconstruction plays | Post-war rebuilding creates multi-year demand in materials, energy, and infrastructure |
| Re-add technology names with supply chain diversification | Companies that have diversified away from conflict zones benefit from recovered demand |

## Key Indicators & Triggers

### Early Warning Signals (Begin Defensive Positioning)
- Simultaneous emergency military exercises by multiple major powers in different theaters (NATO, INDOPACOM)
- Great-power hotline communications severed or ambassador-level diplomatic contacts cut
- UN Security Council rendered paralyzed with multiple vetoes from permanent members in active conflict
- Multiple regional conflicts escalating simultaneously (Europe + Asia + Middle East) within a short timeframe
- Coordinated, state-attributed cyberattacks on financial systems, power grids, or military infrastructure of multiple Western nations
- Rare earth, semiconductor, or critical mineral export bans announced by a major power
- US or allied nations invoking Defense Production Act or equivalent wartime economic legislation
- Nuclear posture changes: strategic forces readiness change, tactical nuclear weapon movement, DEFCON status update

### Escalation Confirmation Signals (Full Defensive Positioning)
- Direct military exchange between armed forces of two or more major powers (US, NATO, China, Russia)
- NATO Article 5 invocation or US mutual defense treaty (ANZUS, US-Japan Treaty) activation
- Emergency UN General Assembly special session convened to address great-power conflict
- Major global exchange circuit breakers triggered simultaneously across multiple markets
- Emergency G7 joint economic statement invoking wartime financial coordination
- Shipping lane closures in two or more global chokepoints (Strait of Hormuz, Strait of Malacca, Suez Canal, Red Sea)
- Mass mobilization / conscription announcements by one or more major powers
- Strategic nuclear forces placed on heightened alert by US, Russia, or China

### De-escalation / Resolution Signals (Reduce Defensive Positioning)
- Formal ceasefire or armistice agreements signed by major combatants with international monitoring
- Great-power diplomatic communication channels re-established (hotlines restored, ambassadors returned)
- International trade routes reopening; shipping insurance rates normalizing
- US and allied nations scaling back Defense Production Act emergency measures
- Strategic nuclear forces returning to normal alert posture
- Global equity markets reopening and stabilizing after conflict-driven closure or halts
- Post-war reconstruction frameworks announced (Marshall Plan-style international agreements)
