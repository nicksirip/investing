## Understanding the Conflict Spectrum

US/China tensions range across a spectrum from routine diplomatic friction to full economic decoupling or military confrontation. Positioning should shift gradually as conditions evolve.

| Level | Description | Example Events |
|-------|-------------|----------------|
| **1 – Low** | Background tension, normal trade | Routine tariff disputes, rhetorical disagreements |
| **2 – Elevated** | Active trade restrictions | New tariff rounds, export control expansions |
| **3 – High** | Targeted sanctions / decoupling | Entity list additions, investment bans, financial sanctions |
| **4 – Critical** | Military signaling / blockade threat | Taiwan Strait exercises, naval confrontations |
| **5 – Hot** | Active military conflict | Direct engagement, blockade of Taiwan |

## Key Risk Factors to Monitor

### Geopolitical & Diplomatic
- US-China summit meetings and communiqués
- Taiwan arms sale announcements
- South China Sea / Taiwan Strait military activity (PACOM advisories)
- State Department travel warnings for China/Hong Kong

### Trade & Economic
- US Trade Representative (USTR) tariff announcements
- Commerce Department Entity List updates
- CHIPS Act enforcement actions
- Chinese retaliatory tariffs or export restrictions on critical minerals (rare earths, gallium, germanium)

### Financial / Market
- CNY/USD exchange rate movement (sharp devaluation = escalation signal)
- Hong Kong Hang Seng Index vs. S&P 500 spread
- Credit default swap spreads on Chinese sovereign debt
- US Treasury foreign holdings data (monthly)

## Individual Stock Considerations

### Long Candidates

**Defense:**
- **LMT (Lockheed Martin)** – F-35 production, HIMARS, missile systems; direct beneficiary of INDOPACOM buildup
- **RTX (Raytheon Technologies)** – Patriot systems, air defense; Taiwan/Japan sales pipeline
- **HII (Huntington Ingalls)** – Only builder of nuclear aircraft carriers; navy expansion play

**Semiconductors (Domestic):**
- **INTC (Intel)** – Only US-based leading-edge fab; CHIPS Act direct beneficiary
- **ACLS (Axcelis Technologies)** – Ion implant equipment; less China-exposed than ASML/AMAT

**Critical Materials:**
- **MP Materials** – Only US rare earth mine and processing; DoD contract support

**Cybersecurity:**
- **CRWD (CrowdStrike)** – Government/defense contracts; nation-state threat detection leader

### Short / Avoid Candidates

- **AAPL** – Significant revenue from Greater China (verify current mix); manufacturing concentration risk
- **NVDA** – Data center chip export restrictions tighten with each escalation
- **QCOM** – High China revenue exposure (verify current mix); Huawei relationship uncertainty
- **BABA / JD / PDD** – Delisting risk, ADR structure risk, Chinese regulatory risk

## Hedging Strategies

### Options-Based Hedges

1. **Put spreads on KWEB** – Buy ATM put / sell OTM put; defined-risk hedge on China tech selloff
2. **MCHI put options** – 3–6 month tenor puts at 5–10% OTM; relatively cheap during low-volatility periods
3. **Taiwan-linked hedge via TSM puts** – TSM is the most direct financial proxy for Taiwan conflict risk
4. **SPY put spreads** – Broad market hedge for systemic risk spill-over

### Currency Hedges

- **Long USD / Short CNH** – via FXI puts, USD/CNH futures, or CNH currency ETFs
- **Long JPY** – Japan is a safe-haven during Asia-Pacific tensions (historically)
- **Long CHF** – Traditional safe-haven currency

### Volatility Plays

- **Long VIX calls or VIX call spreads** – Escalation events cause sharp VIX spikes
- **Long UVXY** – Leveraged VIX ETF for short-term escalation events (decay makes this a short-term trade only)

## Scenario-Based Positioning

### Scenario A: Tariff Escalation (Level 2)

**Probability driver:** USTR tariff announcement, retaliatory tariffs

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Add XLE, ITA | Energy security, defense spending |
| Trim AAPL, QCOM | Direct tariff earnings impact |
| Add GLD 5–10% allocation | Flight to safety |
| Short KWEB via puts | Chinese tech impacted by tariff uncertainty |

### Scenario B: Export Controls / Entity List Expansion (Level 3)

**Probability driver:** BIS/Commerce Dept. announcements, CHIPS enforcement

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Add REMX, MP Materials | Supply chain diversification urgency |
| Trim NVDA, AVGO | Chip export restriction headwinds |
| Add CIBR | Cyber escalation |
| Reduce China ADR exposure to near zero | Regulatory and sanctions risk |

### Scenario C: Taiwan Strait Military Escalation (Level 4)

**Probability driver:** PLA military exercises, PACOM advisories, naval incidents

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Increase ITA, XAR to 10–15% | Defense spending surge expected |
| Add HII, LMT, RTX | Direct naval/air defense beneficiaries |
| Reduce TSM, AAPL exposure significantly | Fab concentration risk, supply chain |
| Long GLD, short EEM | Risk-off, EM contagion |
| Buy VIX calls (30–60 day) | Volatility spike hedge |
| Long JPY, CHF | Safe-haven currencies |

### Scenario D: De-escalation / Diplomatic Reset (Level 1)

**Probability driver:** Presidential summit, trade deal framework, ceasefire language

| Action | Rationale |
|--------|-----------|
| Trim defense overweights | Peace dividend; sector rotation |
| Selectively re-add China tech (BABA, JD) | Depressed valuations; sentiment recovery |
| Add MCHI/KWEB call spreads | Asymmetric upside on diplomatic thaw |
| Reduce GLD | Risk-on; safe havens deflate |

## Key Indicators & Triggers

### Early Warning Signals (Add Defensive Positioning)
- PLA conducting major Taiwan Strait live-fire exercises
- US Treasury sanctions on Chinese financial institutions
- China export restrictions on rare earths (gallium, germanium, antimony)
- Congressional passage of new China-related trade legislation
- CNY depreciating more than 3% in a month

### De-escalation Signals (Reduce Defensive Positioning)
- US-China presidential or treasury-level bilateral meetings scheduled
- Joint communiqués referencing "constructive dialogue"
- Removal of entities from Commerce Dept. Entity List
- Resumption of military-to-military communication channels
